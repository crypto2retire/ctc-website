What Do Junk Removal Companies Take? (Complete List)

Quick Answer: Clear the Clutter removes furniture, appliances, electronics, yard waste, construction debris, and miscellaneous household items. We do NOT take hazardous materials (paint, chemicals, asbestos), medical waste, or items containing hazardous substances. When in doubt, call us at 920-424-9827.

Before scheduling junk removal in Oshkosh or Appleton, many people wonder: "Will they take my stuff?" This guide provides a complete breakdown of what Clear the Clutter accepts, what we don't take and why, and what happens to your items after we pick them up.

Furniture & Household Items We Accept

  • Couches, sofas, and sectionals
  • Beds, mattresses, and box springs
  • Dressers, nightstands, and bedroom furniture
  • Dining tables, chairs, and hutches
  • Bookshelves, cabinets, and storage units
  • Desks, office chairs, and office furniture
  • Coffee tables, side tables, and accent furniture
  • Recliners, armchairs, and upholstered seating
  • Doors, windows, and architectural elements
  • Exercise equipment and gym machines
  • Boxes, bags, and general household clutter

Appliances We Accept

  • Refrigerators and freezers
  • Washing machines and dryers
  • Ovens, stoves, and ranges
  • Dishwashers
  • Microwaves
  • Water heaters
  • Air conditioners and HVAC units
  • Space heaters
  • Portable appliances (blenders, toasters, coffee makers, if non-functional)

Note: We properly handle refrigerants (Freon) in old appliances. There may be special handling fees for appliances, typically $25-$75 per unit.

Electronics & Tech We Accept

  • TVs (all sizes and types)
  • Computers, laptops, and monitors
  • Printers and office equipment
  • Stereos, speakers, and audio equipment
  • Game consoles
  • Keyboards and peripherals
  • Cables, wires, and tech clutter

Important: Electronics are screened for data and recycled at certified e-waste facilities. We never send electronics to landfills.

Yard Waste & Outdoor Items We Accept

  • Branches, brush, and trimmed trees
  • Leaves and yard debris
  • Mulch piles
  • Old fencing and wood pallets
  • Hot tubs and spas (disassembled)
  • Sheds and small structures (removal)
  • Outdoor furniture (broken or unwanted)
  • Grills and outdoor equipment

Construction & Renovation Debris

  • Drywall and plaster
  • Lumber and wood scraps
  • Flooring materials
  • Roofing shingles
  • Windows and doors (old)
  • Concrete and brick (small amounts)
  • Metal studs and framing

What We DON'T Take (And Why)

Hazardous Materials

We cannot accept items that pose environmental or health risks:

  • Paint and paint cans (even if sealed)
  • Chemicals and cleaning supplies
  • Gasoline, propane, and fuel
  • Pesticides and herbicides
  • Asbestos-containing materials
  • Batteries (single-use; rechargeable in moderation)
  • Oil and grease

Why: Hazardous materials require special disposal at licensed facilities. Mixing them with regular junk violates environmental law and creates safety risks.

Medical & Biohazard Waste

  • Medical sharps (needles, syringes)
  • Blood-soaked materials
  • Medications and pharmaceutical waste
  • Expired medical equipment

Tires & Automotive Waste

  • Used tires (full-service tire shops handle these)
  • Engine oil and transmission fluid
  • Car parts and automotive debris

Large Appliances With Unknown Contents

If an appliance contains unknown hazardous materials or is damaged in a way that poses risk, we may decline it. Call ahead if unsure.

What Happens to Your Items After Pickup

Donation

Usable furniture, household goods, and items in working condition are donated to:

  • Clear the Clutter's retail store in Oshkosh (furniture, clothing, books, kitchenware)
  • Local charities and nonprofits in the Fox Valley
  • Schools and community organizations

You can request a donation receipt for tax purposes.

Recycling

Items sent for recycling include:

  • Electronics (e-waste facilities)
  • Scrap metal (metal recycling centers)
  • Wood and lumber (composting or biomass)
  • Yard waste (mulching)
  • Cardboard and paper (paper mills)

Responsible Disposal

Items not suitable for donation or recycling are disposed of at licensed landfills or waste facilities, never illegally dumped.

Items That Require Advance Notice

Call us before scheduling if you have:

  • Large appliances (refrigerators, water heaters)
  • Hot tubs or large spa equipment
  • Old propane or fuel tanks
  • Extensive construction debris (may need separate estimates)
  • Items over 15 feet long
  • Items requiring special disassembly or handling

How to Prepare Your Items for Pickup

Do

  • Group items together in one area for faster loading
  • Empty drawers and cabinets beforehand
  • Mention any items containing hazardous materials
  • Clear pathways so our crew can access items
  • Remove valuables or items you want kept

Don't

  • Don't mix hazardous materials with regular junk
  • Don't leave items in hard-to-access places
  • Don't expect us to take items not mentioned during estimate
  • Don't store items unsafely (blocking exits, unstable piles)
